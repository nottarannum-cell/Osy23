Common Hair Care Mistakes

Avoid these common pitfalls for healthier hair

Using Too Much Oil

Applying excessive amounts of oil can lead to product buildup, greasiness, and clogged pores on the scalp.

Fix: Start with small amounts (a few drops), focus on ends and mid-lengths, and avoid the scalp unless treating dryness. Less is often more!

Overwashing

Applying Conditioner on Scalp

Putting conditioner directly on your scalp can clog pores, cause buildup, and lead to scalp issues like dandruff or irritation.

Fix: Apply conditioner from mid-lengths to ends only. Your scalp produces its own natural oils and doesn't need heavy conditioners.

Using Heavy Oils on Low Porosity Hair

Low porosity hair has tightly closed cuticles, so heavy oils like castor or olive oil sit on the surface, causing buildup and making hair feel greasy.

Fix: Use lightweight oils like jojoba, grapeseed, or argan oil. Apply to damp hair and use heat to help open cuticles for better absorption.

Too Much Protein

Overusing protein treatments can make hair brittle, stiff, and prone to breakage, especially if your hair doesn't need it.

Fix: Balance protein with moisture. High porosity hair needs more protein, while low porosity hair needs less. Use protein treatments 1-2√ó/month unless your hair specifically needs more.

Hot Water

Washing with very hot water can strip natural oils, cause frizz, increase dryness, and damage the hair cuticle.

Fix: Use lukewarm or cool water for washing and rinsing. Cool water helps seal the cuticle, reducing frizz and adding shine.

Brushing Wet Hair Aggressively

Hair is weakest when wet. Rough brushing can cause breakage, split ends, and damage the hair shaft.

Fix: Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush. Start from ends and work your way up. Be gentle and patient, especially with tangles.

Not Rinsing Conditioner Properly

Leaving conditioner residue can cause buildup, make hair look greasy, and weigh it down.

Fix: Rinse thoroughly with cool water until hair feels clean and smooth. If using leave-in conditioner, use a small amount and only on ends.

Remember

Hair care is personal and what works for one person may not work for another. Pay attention to how your hair responds and adjust your routine accordingly.

If you experience severe scalp issues, persistent hair loss, or other concerning symptoms, consult a dermatologist for professional advice.
